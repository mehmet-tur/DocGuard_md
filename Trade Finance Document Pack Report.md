# **Domain Workflow & Document Pack Report**

## **1\. Workflow Map with Specific Actors**

The architectural foundation of the Turkish trade finance ecosystem represents a sophisticated intersection of advanced digital transformation initiatives and highly regulated financial mechanisms. Rooted deeply in the legislative frameworks established by the 6361 Numbered Financial Leasing, Factoring and Financing Companies Law enacted in 2012, the operational environment is governed by stringent oversight from the Banking Regulation and Supervision Agency (BDDK).1 Following the consolidation of the Factoring Association into the Finansal Kurumlar Birliği in 2013, the sector formalized a rigorous operational standard that mandates precise choreography between commercial entities, financial institutions, and governmental digital infrastructure.1 At the core of this ecosystem, the DocGuard (BelgeKalkanı) evidence engine operates to ensure the mathematical, structural, and chronological integrity of the underlying commercial documentation.  
To comprehensively understand the deployment environment of the BelgeKalkanı engine, it is imperative to dissect the distinct workflows and define the specific obligations, constraints, and operational mechanisms of every participating actor. The commercial finance landscape in Turkey bifurcates into two primary, fundamentally distinct streams: Traditional Factoring, which relies on the legal mechanism of assignment of receivables (alacak devri), and Participation Banking (Katılım Bankası) mechanisms, specifically Islamic trade finance executed via Murabaha contracts.

### **Core Actors in the Ecosystem**

The ecosystem operates through the synchronized interactions of a clearly defined set of institutional and commercial actors, each bearing specific legal, technical, and financial responsibilities.2

1. **Satıcı (Seller/Supplier/Exporter):** The commercial enterprise initiating the foundational trade activity. The Satıcı supplies goods or services, generates the primary commercial documents—most notably the electronic invoice (e-Fatura) and the electronic despatch advice (e-İrsaliye)—and seeks immediate working capital liquidity by presenting these documents to a financial institution.2  
2. **Alıcı (Buyer/Debtor/Importer):** The corporate recipient of the commercial goods or services. In conventional factoring arrangements, this entity represents the ultimate source of payment and is liable for settling the invoice value at the agreed-upon maturity date.2  
3. **Faktor (Factoring Company / Conventional Financial Institution):** The specialized financial institution that accepts the legal transfer of the commercial receivable (alacak devri) from the Satıcı. The Faktor provides an immediate advance payment—typically scaling up to 80-90% of the total invoice value—and assumes the administrative mandate for final collection from the Alıcı at maturity.4  
4. **Katılım Bankası (Participation Bank):** The Islamic financial institution facilitating the Murabaha transaction. In strict contrast to a conventional Faktor, the Katılım Bankası cannot simply finance a pre-existing debt instrument. To comply with Shariah principles, the bank must temporarily acquire actual legal title to the underlying commercial goods from the Satıcı before executing a subsequent resale to the Alıcı at a predetermined, transparent markup.6  
5. **Gelir İdaresi Başkanlığı (GİB \- Revenue Administration):** The ultimate governmental authority orchestrating the mandatory e-transformation architecture across the Turkish digital economy. GİB maintains the central regulatory portal through which all structured UBL-TR XML electronic documents must pass for foundational technical validation and centralized routing.3  
6. **Özel Entegratör (Special Integrator):** Authorized third-party technology service providers acting as the critical technical bridge between private enterprise ERP systems (such as SAP, Oracle, or local accounting software) and the state-operated GİB portal. They handle the complex cryptographic signing, envelope generation, and conversion of raw commercial data into the mandated UBL-TR XML formats.8  
7. **Credit Insurer (Kredi Sigorta Şirketi):** An optional, yet highly prevalent, institutional actor that conducts extensive due diligence on the Alıcı's corporate health, determining a viable credit ceiling, and providing financial protection to the Faktor or Katılım Bankası against the potential default of the ultimate debtor.2

### **Workflow A: Conventional Factoring (Alacak Devri)**

The conventional factoring workflow is a highly structured sequence of established administrative, legal, and financial procedures engineered to convert short-term commercial debt into immediate operational liquidity. This process relies entirely on the evaluation of an unbroken chain of documentation proving both the physical delivery of tangible goods and the unencumbered legal transfer of the associated financial claim.

1. **Başvuru (Application) and Institutional Onboarding:** The operational cycle initiates when the Satıcı, encountering a requirement for immediate cash flow, approaches the Faktor. The Satıcı submits a comprehensive application package incorporating foundational commercial indicators. This package includes historical commercial data, potentially post-dated commercial checks, proforma invoices mapping future trade, and strictly requires the completion of the Tüzel Kişi Müşteri Bilgi Formu (Corporate Customer Information Form) to establish foundational identity and signature authorities.9  
2. **Entity Evaluation and Limit Allocation:** The Faktor executes an extensive evaluation of the commercial history, sector stability, and historical payment behaviors of the targeted Alıcı. Upon a successful evaluation, the institution formalizes a Limit Onay Bildirimi (LOB \- Limit Approval Notification). This critical internal document mathematically defines the Azami Faktoring Hacmi (AFH \- Maximum Factoring Volume) and the specific Borçlu İşlem Hacmi (BİH \- Debtor Transaction Volume), establishing the absolute financial boundaries the Faktor is willing to tolerate for that specific supply chain relationship.4  
3. **Physical Fulfillment and e-İrsaliye Generation:** Prior to any physical movement of the commercial goods, the Satıcı must interact with their Özel Entegratör to generate an e-İrsaliye. This UBL-TR formatted XML document encapsulates granular transport details, carrier identification, exact volumetric measurements, and specific line-item commodity descriptions. Once cryptographically sealed, it is transmitted through the GİB infrastructure to the Alıcı, establishing a verifiable, time-stamped record of physical transit.8  
4. **Commercial Realization and e-Fatura Issuance:** Following the successful physical delivery and acceptance of the goods by the Alıcı, the commercial transaction reaches formalization via the generation of an e-Fatura. To maintain a rigorous, unbroken audit trail, this electronic invoice must explicitly reference the previously generated physical transport document by embedding the exact cbc:ID of the e-İrsaliye within its cac:DespatchDocumentReference data node.12  
5. **Assignment of Receivables (Temlik) Execution:** To transform the commercial invoice into a financial asset, the Satıcı formally transfers the rights of the receivable to the Faktor. This fundamental legal mechanism, established as alacak devri, is documented and executed via the physical or digital signing of the Alacak Bildirim Formu (AB-NTR \- Receivable Notification Form). The Satıcı supplies the Faktor with the finalized UBL-TR e-Fatura files alongside the completed AB-NTR, which explicitly lists the assigned invoice numbers and their corresponding monetary values.4  
6. **Documentary Validation and Disbursement (Ön Ödeme):** The operational back-office of the Faktor, typically heavily reliant on Client Administrators, conducts extensive manual or semi-automated cross-referencing across the submitted document pack to confirm structural and mathematical consistency. Upon successful validation of the data integrity, the Faktor executes the preliminary financial disbursement (ön ödeme)—transferring the agreed-upon percentage of the invoice value directly to the verified IBAN of the Satıcı.4  
7. **Maturity Cycle and Final Settlement (Tahsilat):** At the legally defined maturity date of the commercial invoice, the Alıcı bypasses the Satıcı and settles the total payable amount directly into the accounts of the Faktor. The Faktor retains the previously advanced capital, deducts its operational commissions and financing fees, and subsequently remits any remaining residual balance directly to the Satıcı, thereby closing the financial loop.2

### **Workflow B: Participation Banking Murabaha (Islamic Trade Finance)**

The Murabaha workflow introduces profound, foundational structural differences compared to conventional factoring, necessitated by the strict requirement for total adherence to Islamic Fiqh and Shariah principles. Conventional trade finance products routinely contain non-compliant elements, most notably the charging of interest (riba) and the outright commercialization or sale of pre-existing debt (Bay' al-Dayn), both of which are explicitly prohibited within this framework.14 Consequently, Murabaha operates not as the financing or discounting of an existing invoice, but as a meticulously structured, direct commercial sale wherein the financial institution acts as an active participant in the physical supply chain.

1. **Procurement Identification and Vekalet (Proxy) Establishment:** The workflow begins when the Alıcı identifies a necessary commercial asset or raw material offered by a specific Satıcı but lacks the immediate liquidity to execute a direct purchase. The Alıcı approaches the Katılım Bankası requesting Murabaha financing. Crucially, the transaction is immediately invalidated if there is a pre-existing, finalized, and binding sales contract already executed between the Satıcı and the Alıcı for this specific, identifiable good.6 Given that modern banks do not maintain physical warehouses for diverse commercial goods, the bank typically establishes a vekalet (proxy/agency) agreement. This legal instrument officially appoints the Alıcı as the bank’s authorized purchasing agent, empowering them to negotiate terms with the Satıcı strictly on behalf of the Katılım Bankası.6  
2. **Asset Acquisition and Title Transfer to Katılım Bankası:** To comply with Fiqh, the Katılım Bankası must verifiably acquire the legal title and ownership of the goods from the Satıcı. The validity of the subsequent financial arrangement hinges entirely on the bank holding actual ownership—and thereby assuming the associated commercial liabilities of ownership—before executing any resale. The bank purchases the goods and remits payment directly to the Satıcı.7 The documentation at this critical stage includes the initial commercial invoice issued by the Satıcı, billed explicitly to the Katılım Bankası, alongside foundational transport and transfer documents.  
3. **Execution of the Murabaha Satış Sözleşmesi:** Only after legal ownership is irrefutably established does the Katılım Bankası execute the Murabaha Satış Sözleşmesi (Murabaha Sales Contract) with the Alıcı. This definitive contract must explicitly and transparently state the original procurement cost of the asset and clearly delineate the agreed-upon profit margin (kâr payı) added by the bank.6  
4. **Transfer of Physical Goods and Documentary Submission:** The physical goods are then transferred to the possession of the Alıcı. A highly complex document pack must be submitted to the bank's operational teams to validate the complete cycle. This pack includes the final commercial fatura, the fully executed Murabaha sales contract, relevant title deeds (tapu) if the asset is real estate, and the associated e-İrsaliye detailing the physical movement.6  
5. **Deferred Settlement Cycle:** The Alıcı settles the total negotiated contract price (the original cost plus the bank's markup) with the Katılım Bankası according to an agreed-upon, deferred installment schedule. It is a critical compliance mandate that the failure to deliver the required underlying commercial documents (proving the actual existence and transfer of the goods) within the strictly stipulated timeframes renders the entire transaction non-compliant with participation banking standards, necessitating immediate corrective action.6

## **3\. Pain Point List with Automation Ratings**

Despite the widespread institutional adoption of mandatory e-transformation tools orchestrated by the Gelir İdaresi Başkanlığı, the operational back-offices of conventional factoring companies and participation banks remain heavily burdened by legacy, paper-based review processes. The operational requirement to integrate highly structured, machine-readable XML data arrays with fundamentally unstructured physical documents (such as legal contracts or handwritten assignment forms) creates profound, systemic operational friction. The manual review process for Client Administrators is notoriously time-consuming, mentally exhausting, and highly susceptible to error.25  
During peak commercial periods, such as quarter-end closings or seasonal inventory shifts, the sheer volume of complex documentation stretches administrative resources to their absolute limits. An expected rise in corporate sales legitimizes an apparent increase in borrowing requests, creating an environment where manual review fatigue leads to critical oversights slipping through the cracks.25 Industry practitioners quietly acknowledge that a baseline of approximately 5% of all financed invoices possess significant "administrative deficiencies"—a broad categorization encompassing everything from simple clerical transposition errors in data entry to sophisticated, intentional manipulations and fabrications of the commercial narrative.26  
The following catalog exhaustively details the primary operational pain points experienced in Turkish trade finance workflows. Each pain point is accompanied by a deep analysis of the operational friction, the mechanism of the vulnerability, and a precise assessment of its viability for automated remediation via the BelgeKalkanı engine.

### **Pain Point 1: The XML to PDF Asymmetry in Alacak Devri Processing**

* **Operational Context:** The fundamental legal act that breathes life into a factoring transaction is the assignment of receivables, executed physically or digitally via the Alacak Bildirim Formu (AB-NTR).11 This is almost universally a physical paper form or a flattened, scanned PDF document completed manually by the Satıcı's accounting department. Client Administrators sit at terminals, forced to manually read complex invoice numbers and monetary amounts from this static PDF, and subsequently cross-reference them against the visual representations (the HTML or PDF rendered stylesheets) of the actual UBL-TR e-Fatura XML files.  
* **Mechanism of Friction:** Human operators are profoundly prone to transposition errors when forced to read and re-type 16-character alphanumeric GİB invoice identifiers (e.g., GIB2009000000011).13 Furthermore, visually verifying that the legal entity named in text on the AB-NTR perfectly matches the underlying cac:AccountingSupplierParty/cac:PartyName/cbc:Name buried in the XML is subjective and highly prone to fatigue-induced errors over a multi-hour shift.13  
* **Automation Rating: 95% (Exceptional Viability).** The BelgeKalkanı engine completely neutralizes this bottleneck by utilizing deterministic XML parsing to extract the exact, cryptographically sealed cbc:ID and PayableAmount directly from the raw e-Fatura envelope, bypassing the visual stylesheet entirely. Simultaneously, it deploys targeted OCR to structure the manually entered data from the AB-NTR. The subsequent mathematical and string-matching comparison achieves near-instantaneous, perfect fidelity, entirely removing the human cognitive bottleneck from the data entry phase.25

### **Pain Point 2: Chronological Despatch and Invoice Timestamp Misalignment**

* **Operational Context:** Foundational commercial logic and strict Turkish tax regulations dictate an unyielding chronological sequence: commercial goods are prepared, an e-İrsaliye is issued to document the freight, the goods are physically shipped, and subsequently, an e-Fatura is issued referencing the completed physical movement to demand payment.12  
* **Mechanism of Friction:** Reviewing the chronological validity of this sequence requires administrators to open multiple disparate files simultaneously. They must locate the cbc:IssueDate buried within the e-İrsaliye 13, hold that date in memory, and compare it against the cbc:IssueDate on the e-Fatura, while also verifying that the exact 16-character ID inside the cac:DespatchDocumentReference bridges the two documents correctly. In complex, modern supply chains involving partial deliveries across multiple weeks, a single consolidated e-Fatura might contain an array of multiple cac:DespatchDocumentReference nodes pointing to numerous e-İrsaliye documents. Manual reconciliation of this web of dates is an administrative nightmare that frequently results in skipped checks.  
* **Automation Rating: 90% (High Viability).** Programmatic extraction effortlessly handles complex one-to-many relationship mapping. The engine extracts all embedded cac:DespatchDocumentReference/cbc:IssueDate arrays from the single e-Fatura and systematically compares them against the standalone array of e-İrsaliye files submitted in the pack. It generates an immediate evidence log of any chronological impossibilities—for instance, highlighting a scenario where a financial invoice claims goods were physically shipped on a date that occurs *after* the invoice itself was finalized.12

### **Pain Point 3: Granular Line-Item Verification in Murabaha Compliance**

* **Operational Context:** Islamic Murabaha transactions strictly require the Katılım Bankası to purchase and resell a specific, clearly identifiable physical asset.6 The documentation pack must provide unbroken proof that the exact commercial goods specified in the legal Murabaha Satış Sözleşmesi were the exact identical goods transacted via the e-Fatura, and the exact identical goods transported via the e-İrsaliye or international Konşimento (Bill of Lading).6  
* **Mechanism of Friction:** Mismatched product descriptions between a commercial invoice and a bill of lading, or differing volumetric quantities across documents, represent severe non-compliance with fundamental Shariah Fiqh standards regarding the certainty of the traded asset.7 Client Administrators are forced to read through dense, highly technical, and often codified commodity classifications (e.g., extracting meaning from cac:CommodityClassification/cbc:ItemClassificationCode or generic terms like "Network cable") 21 and manually verify their semantic equivalence against paragraphs of unstructured natural language buried within legal contracts.  
* **Automation Rating: 80% (Substantial Viability).** While the programmatic XML extraction of the exact text within cac:InvoiceLine/cac:Item/cbc:Name is trivial 21, bridging the gap by mapping these specific, often abbreviated commercial terms against the natural language of a scanned Murabaha contract requires highly advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP). The BelgeKalkanı engine utilizes specialized Large Language Models (LLMs) specifically trained on Turkish commercial and legal terminology to mathematically assess the semantic equivalence of line items across these highly disparate formats, significantly accelerating a review process that previously required deep domain expertise.24

### **Pain Point 4: Multi-System Silos and Internal Duplicate Presentations**

* **Operational Context:** A persistent vulnerability in supply-chain financing—highlighted globally in high-profile collapses such as the First Brands incident—is the duplicate presentation of identical commercial documents across multiple, uncoordinated financial institutions.26 A malicious actor or an administratively disorganized Satıcı might submit the exact same UBL-TR e-Fatura to Factor A and Factor B simultaneously to secure double liquidity.  
* **Mechanism of Friction:** Because financial institutions operate on highly isolated, proprietary core banking platforms, Factor A has zero visibility into the active workflows of Factor B.27 While consortium-style blockchain or data-sharing initiatives aimed at solving duplicate financing have existed for years, their real-world effectiveness remains severely constrained by low institutional participation rates and integration latency.26 Beyond cross-institutional issues, *internal* duplication—where a client submits a slightly visually altered invoice or re-submits an older, already financed invoice to a different branch within the same institution—is incredibly challenging to catch when relying on manual ledger checks across disparate internal software modules.  
* **Automation Rating: 100% (Absolute Viability \- Internal Scope).** While cross-institutional visibility remains structurally impossible without consortium databases, within the strictly internal boundaries of a single institution, BelgeKalkanı achieves absolute, perfect automation. By generating an immutable cryptographic hash combining the cbc:UUID of the UBL-TR envelope and indexing it alongside the specific cbc:ID and the cac:AccountingSupplierParty VKN, the engine immediately identifies any internal re-presentation of an identical digital asset, instantly eliminating internal duplication vulnerabilities.18

### **Pain Point 5: Mathematical Reconciliations of Complex Tax and Line Extensions**

* **Operational Context:** The final PayableAmount stated at the bottom of a commercial e-Fatura is not a static number; it is the mathematical culmination of numerous complex sub-calculations. This includes aggregating individual cbc:LineExtensionAmount values for every single item, applying various overarching cac:TaxTotal modifiers, and accounting for highly specific tax exemptions denoted by complex arrays of cbc:TaxExemptionReasonCode parameters.21  
* **Mechanism of Friction:** Fabricated or heavily manipulated documents often exhibit profound mathematical impossibilities. An actor might use a PDF editor to artificially inflate the final visual total of an invoice to secure a larger factoring advance, but fail to correctly recalculate and update the underlying XML line items to match the new total.26 Manually recalculating these complex, multi-line tax structures using desktop calculators is entirely unfeasible for high-volume processing environments.  
* **Automation Rating: 100% (Absolute Viability).** The engine bypasses human arithmetic entirely by automatically recalculating the entire mathematical tree of the UBL-TR XML structure from the ground up. It aggregates every single cac:InvoiceLine/cbc:LineExtensionAmount, applies the strictly documented cbc:Percent multipliers found within the ClassifiedTaxCategory, and rigorously verifies the final computed result against the explicitly stated cac:LegalMonetaryTotal/cbc:PayableAmount.16 Any deviation down to the final decimal point is instantly logged.

## **4\. Anomaly Pattern Catalog (Internal Comparisons Only)**

The core analytical architecture of the BelgeKalkanı evidence engine is fundamentally predicated on its capacity to identify structural, mathematical, and logical anomalies by treating the submitted document pack as a completely closed, strictly interrelated system. The engine operates on the principle of intrinsic consistency; it does not rely on pinging external governmental databases, it does not query third-party credit consortium data, and it does not scrape the open web. Rather, it performs a forensic, multidimensional cross-examination of the documents provided entirely within the boundaries of a single application dossier.  
Discrepancies identified within this closed loop serve as highly reliable, deterministic indicators of administrative deficiency, institutional data entry error, or intentional commercial manipulation. The following catalog strictly defines the exact patterns of inconsistency identified by the engine's internal comparison algorithms.

### **Pattern Alpha: Cryptographic and Standard Envelope Deviations**

This pattern focuses entirely on evaluating the foundational structural integrity of the electronic documents themselves, specifically focusing on the required metadata within the UBL-TR 1.2 XML envelopes mandated by the GİB.

* **Version and Customization Framework Mismatches:** The engine's first operation evaluates the cbc:UBLVersionID and cbc:CustomizationID declarations. Within the highly regulated Turkish ecosystem, valid invoices must explicitly declare the regional customization schema (e.g., the exact string \<cbc:CustomizationID\>TR1.2\</cbc:CustomizationID\>).13 The engine generates a structural anomaly marker if these critical declarations are missing, malformed, or point to outdated/unrecognized international schemas, providing strong evidence that the document was likely generated outside the authorized, secure Özel Entegratör ecosystem.  
* **Cryptographic UUID Asymmetry:** Every legitimate e-Fatura and e-İrsaliye generated in Turkey possesses a Universally Unique Identifier permanently embedded within the cbc:UUID node.13 The engine extracts this cryptographic string and cross-references it against the visual representation of the UUID printed on the PDF rendering of the invoice. If the OCR-extracted visual UUID diverges by even a single character from the cryptographic UUID embedded in the XML code, the system logs absolute, deterministic evidence of digital document tampering, indicating the visual layer was manipulated independently of the data layer.

### **Pattern Beta: Identity and Entity Asymmetry**

This pattern focuses on ensuring that the corporate actors defined in the underlying commercial transaction strictly and legally mirror the actors defined in the financial and legal assignment transaction.

* **VKN / TCKN Misalignment Markers:** The fundamental, unalterable identifier in Turkish commercial and tax law is the Vergi Kimlik Numarası (VKN) or the Türkiye Cumhuriyeti Kimlik Numarası (TCKN).3 The engine navigates to extract the string value from cbc:ID schemeID="VKN" nested within the cac:AccountingSupplierParty/cac:PartyIdentification node.13 This precise value is then strictly, character-for-character, compared against the applicant's VKN extracted via OCR from the physical Tüzel Kişi Müşteri Bilgi Formu 10 and the Alacak Bildirim Formu (AB-NTR).11 Any string deviation logged here indicates severe institutional misalignment, providing evidence that the entity attempting to assign the financial receivable is not the legal issuer of the foundational commercial invoice.  
* **Financial Routing (IBAN) Discrepancy:** The cac:PaymentMeans node within a standard e-Fatura frequently contains a cac:PayeeFinancialAccount/cbc:ID parameter, representing the commercial seller's designated IBAN. The engine extracts this UBL-TR IBAN and compares it directly against the payout instructions detailed manually on the AB-NTR.11 Discrepancies identified in this comparison indicate a profound operational vulnerability where advanced funds might be diverted to unauthorized, unverified financial accounts—a known, critical exposure point in rapid factoring operations.25

### **Pattern Gamma: Chronological and Sequential Impossibilities**

This analytical pattern evaluates the temporal logic of the trade cycle, ensuring that the documented events align with the unbending physics of linear time and the strict sequence required by commercial law.

* **Retroactive Despatch Reference Anomalies:** The fundamental workflow of domestic commerce requires goods to be physically documented prior to the issuance of a financial demand.12 The engine extracts the final cbc:IssueDate of the e-Fatura and compares it directly to the cbc:IssueDate found deeply embedded within the cac:DespatchDocumentReference array node.13 If the mathematical comparison reveals that the physical despatch date occurred *after* the invoice issuance date, the engine produces a sequence anomaly marker, indicating an impossible commercial timeline where payment was demanded for a physical movement that had not yet been documented.  
* **Murabaha Title Sequence Violations:** In the strict domain of Islamic trade finance, the Katılım Bankası must verifiably own the asset before the Murabaha resale contract can be legally executed.6 The engine extracts the signature date from the physical Murabaha Satış Sözleşmesi 6 and compares it against the cbc:IssueDate of the commercial e-Fatura billed by the original supplier to the bank. If the contract date between the bank and the client precedes the bank's own purchase date, the transaction violates the foundational Fiqh requirement of ownership. The engine logs this as a severe chronological sequence irregularity, providing evidence of critical Shariah non-compliance.6

### **Pattern Delta: Mathematical and Volumetric Discrepancies**

This pattern conducts automated, comprehensive accounting logic across the entire document pack to identify numerical fabrications.

* **Calculated vs. Stated Total Divergence:** As detailed previously, manually fabricated invoices frequently contain arithmetic errors resulting from the manipulation of visual PDF layers without updating the underlying XML mathematical relationships.26 The engine recalculates the aggregate sum of all individual cac:InvoiceLine/cbc:LineExtensionAmount values 16, accurately applies the relevant cac:TaxTotal modifiers and exemptions 21, and mathematically compares the newly programmatic result against the explicitly stated cac:LegalMonetaryTotal/cbc:PayableAmount.16 Any non-zero variance identified during this recalculation generates a mathematical anomaly record.  
* **Cross-Document Volumetric Quantity Mismatch:** The engine extracts the specific cbc:InvoicedQuantity (including the vital unitCode parameter, such as "C62" indicating individual pieces or "KGM" indicating kilograms) directly from the e-Fatura.21 This precise value is then compared against the cbc:DeliveredQuantity extracted from the associated e-İrsaliye 8 or against the stated gross weights and unit counts extracted via OCR from the international Konşimento (Bill of Lading).22 Irregularities identified between the volume of goods financially invoiced and the volume of goods physically transported provide concrete evidence of inflated billing practices—a common manipulation technique extensively documented in complex supply-chain collapses such as the First Brands incident.26

### **Pattern Epsilon: Semantic and Commodity Mismatches**

This pattern departs from strict mathematics and utilizes advanced Natural Language Processing to evaluate the conceptual descriptions of the goods being financed.

* **Commercial Good Divergence Markers:** The engine utilizes specialized LLMs to evaluate the semantic similarity and conceptual overlap between the short-form string extracted from the cac:InvoiceLine/cac:Item/cbc:Name (e.g., "Cat5 Network cable") 21 and the long-form, unstructured product descriptions listed across the physical Murabaha Satış Sözleşmesi or the maritime Bill of Lading.24 If the structural invoice details heavy industrial machining equipment, but the associated transport document describes the shipment of lightweight consumer electronics or agricultural goods, the engine logs a severe semantic mismatch anomaly. This provides Client Administrators with critical, immediate evidence of fundamentally disconnected trade narratives that require manual investigation.24

## **5\. MVP Scope Boundary**

The BelgeKalkanı evidence engine is meticulously engineered to address the highly specific, high-friction validation requirements inherent to the Turkish trade finance sector. To ensure rapid institutional deployment, maintain exceptionally high computational accuracy, and deliver immediate, measurable operational value, the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) boundary is rigorously, intentionally constrained. The MVP restricts its operational scope to purely internal, document-to-document cross-verifications, relying entirely and exclusively on the static data provided within a specific application dossier at the time of upload.  
It explicitly excludes complex integrations with external third-party databases, it does not connect to cross-institutional consortium networks, and it does not make live, real-time API calls to the GİB backend portals for the initial deployment phase. The specific operational parameters, ingestion limits, and technical processing boundaries of the Phase 1 architecture are comprehensively detailed below.

### **Ingestion and Data Parsing Boundaries**

1. **Structured Data Ingestion Limitations:** The engine will natively parse and process UBL-TR 1.2 XML files for both e-Fatura and e-İrsaliye document types.3 It is programmed to traverse the XML tree to extract only strictly defined, pre-mapped nodes relevant to financial evaluation:  
   * **Envelope Metadata:** Confined to cbc:UBLVersionID, cbc:CustomizationID, cbc:UUID, cbc:IssueDate, and cbc:InvoiceTypeCode.13  
   * **Party Identification:** Confined to cac:AccountingSupplierParty and cac:AccountingCustomerParty, explicitly extracting the cbc:ID schemeID="VKN" and schemeID="TCKN" attributes.13  
   * **Financial Totals:** Confined to navigating the cac:LegalMonetaryTotal array and extracting its critical child node cbc:PayableAmount.16  
   * **Line Items:** Confined to the cac:InvoiceLine array, extracting cbc:InvoicedQuantity, cbc:LineExtensionAmount, and cac:Item/cbc:Name.21  
   * **Document Linkage:** Confined to extracting cac:DespatchDocumentReference/cbc:ID and its associated cbc:IssueDate.13  
2. **Unstructured Data Extraction (OCR/NLP) Limitations:** The engine will accept standard PDF uploads for specific, pre-defined physical documents. The MVP scope strictly limits optical character recognition and natural language processing capabilities to three specific document archetypes:  
   * **Alacak Bildirim Formu (AB-NTR):** Data extraction is rigidly limited to locating and parsing the Applicant Name, Applicant VKN, Invoice Numbers, Invoice Amounts, and the stated IBAN.11  
   * **Tüzel Kişi Müşteri Bilgi Formu:** Data extraction is limited to parsing the Corporate Name and the VKN/TCKN strings.10  
   * **Murabaha Satış Sözleşmesi / Konşimento:** Data extraction is limited to identifying document execution dates, isolating asset/goods descriptions for semantic comparison, and extracting quantitative measurements (such as gross weight or unit counts).6

### **Computational and Validation Boundaries**

The engine's computational core during the MVP phase is restricted to executing deterministic logic gates, mathematical recalculations, and bounded semantic similarity checks.

1. **Deterministic Cross-Referencing Execution:** The engine will perform exact-match string comparisons between the definitively structured XML-extracted VKNs and the probabilistic OCR-extracted VKNs. It will perform exact mathematical comparisons between the XML PayableAmount 16 and the OCR-extracted assignment amounts from the physical AB-NTR forms.  
2. **Internal Duplication Indexing:** The engine will generate an immutable cryptographic hash combining the cbc:UUID and the cac:AccountingSupplierParty/cac:PartyIdentification/cbc:ID 13 for every ingested UBL-TR document. This unique hash will be stored in an isolated, client-specific internal index database to identify if the identical digital invoice is presented multiple times *within the same specific institution's workflow*. Expanding this index for cross-institutional duplicate checking is explicitly outside the MVP boundary.26  
3. **Semantic Similarity Bounding constraints:** The integration of LLMs 24 is restricted solely and exclusively to comparing the extracted text string of cac:InvoiceLine/cac:Item/cbc:Name against the isolated goods description paragraphs found in the transport or Murabaha documents. The LLM will output a strictly numerical confidence percentage regarding the similarity of the described goods. It will not perform broader sentiment analysis, it will not evaluate the legal validity of the contract clauses, and it will not perform external web scraping to verify if the described product actually exists in the broader market.

### **Output and Interface Boundaries**

1. **Evidence Generation Framework:** The primary, singular output of the BelgeKalkanı engine is the generation of a "Document Integrity & Evidence Report." This report is delivered simultaneously as a highly structured JSON payload for backend system consumption and as an accompanying, human-readable PDF summary designed for Client Administrators.  
2. **Strict Constraint on Decision Making:** The architecture dictates that the engine operates strictly and exclusively as an analytical observation tool. It will enumerate every identified mismatch, mathematical error, or chronological impossibility (e.g., logging exact phrases such as "VKN mismatch identified," or "Chronological sequence invalid"). The architecture specifies that the engine will absolutely not generate automated workflow decisions; it will not halt the disbursement of capital, it will not advance a transaction to the next stage, and it will not provide subjective commentary on the viability of the client. The interpretation of the generated evidence log and the subsequent, final execution of the factoring or Murabaha transaction remain entirely within the purview of the human Client Administrators and the institution's established operational hierarchy.  
3. **System Integration Parameters:** The MVP is engineered and deployed as a standalone, modular REST API. Financial institutions will programmatically push the collected document pack (comprising the XMLs and PDFs) to the secure BelgeKalkanı endpoint. The endpoint will process the array and return the Evidence Report payload. Deep, bidirectional integration into legacy core banking mainframes—where the engine might automatically update general ledgers or manipulate existing limit databases—is designated for subsequent, post-MVP architectural phases.
